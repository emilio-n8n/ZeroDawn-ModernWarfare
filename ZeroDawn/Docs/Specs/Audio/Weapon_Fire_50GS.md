# Sound: .50 GS Weapon Fire

## Sound Description
- **Type:** SFX
- **Context:** Plays each time the .50 GS hand cannon is fired. A massive .50 AE semi-automatic pistol with a fire rate of 180 RPM and 7-round magazine.
- **Emotional Quality:** Thunderous, devastating, awe-inspiring — every shot feels like a cannon blast that demands attention and respect.
- **Duration:** 0.35s per shot
- **Tonal Characterization:** The .50 GS produces the most distinctive and intimidating fire sound in the game — a deep, thunderous "BOOM" that is closer to a small explosive charge than a pistol round. The .50 Action Express cartridge generates an enormous muzzle blast with a heavy low-frequency concussion that shakes the audio space. The report has a rich, complex texture: a initial sharp crack of the supersonic round, immediately followed by a massive low-end bloom that resonates for hundreds of milliseconds. The compensator adds a sharp, directional venting hiss on top. The brushed stainless steel slide produces a weighty, resonant metallic clang as it cycles — slower and more deliberate than any other firearm. Each shot sounds monumentally powerful, with an echo that lingers and commands the battlefield.

## Layering Notes
- **Attack:** Massive, explosive transient — the most powerful attack of any weapon
- **Sustain:** Extended (30-50ms) — the sustained roar of the .50 AE round
- **Decay:** Long — 250-350ms of rumbling echo and heavy metal slide cycling
- **Reverb:** Benefits greatly from environmental reverb — sounds devastating in corridors and urban environments
- **Distance modeling:** The low-frequency rumble carries enormous distances — .50 GS fire should be audible across the entire map, serving as an audio signature of the hand cannon's presence
