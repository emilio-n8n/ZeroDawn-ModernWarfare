# Sound: 9mm Pistol Weapon Fire

## Sound Description
- **Type:** SFX
- **Context:** Plays each time the 9mm Pistol is fired. A standard-issue compact semi-automatic sidearm with a fire rate of 400 RPM and 12-round magazine.
- **Emotional Quality:** Quick, snappy, utilitarian — conveys the workmanlike reliability of a standard military sidearm.
- **Duration:** 0.06s per shot
- **Tonal Characterization:** A light, snappy, high-pitched "crack-pop" that is distinctly thinner and shorter than any rifle fire. The 9mm Parabellum round produces a sharp, almost brittle report with minimal low-end presence. The polymer frame contributes a plastic, non-resonant quality to the sound — no deep metallic ringing, just a quick mechanical slap from the slide cycling. The report has a distinctive "spit" quality that sounds compact and contained. In rapid semi-auto fire, the individual shots are fast and crisp — each pop clear and distinct, conveying the lighter recoil and quick recovery of a pistol platform. The slide lock sound on the final round provides a useful audio cue.

## Layering Notes
- **Attack:** Very fast, sharp transient — high-frequency dominant
- **Sustain:** Minimal (5-10ms) — the supersonic crack of a small-caliber round
- **Decay:** Very short — 40-50ms, the slide cycling provides the tail
- **Reverb:** Minimal — sounds close and intimate, ideal for first-person perspective
- **Distance modeling:** Falls off quickly at range — the high-pitched crack dissipates faster than rifle fire
