# Sound: M4A1 Weapon Fire

## Sound Description
- **Type:** SFX
- **Context:** Plays each time the M4A1 assault rifle is fired. The M4A1 is a balanced, accurate 5.56mm carbine with a moderate fire rate of 700 RPM.
- **Emotional Quality:** Satisfying, precise, controlled — conveys the feeling of a well-engineered military carbine delivering accurate suppressive fire.
- **Duration:** 0.12s per shot
- **Tonal Characterization:** A crisp, sharp crack followed by a brief metallic ringing from the bolt carrier group cycling. The report is moderate in pitch — not as deep as the AK-47 nor as high as the CQB-9. A clean muzzle blast with a tight, directional punch that suggests range and accuracy. The sound has a slight "paper tear" quality from the supersonic round, with a resonant metallic slap as the bolt slams forward between shots. In full-auto, the individual cracks blend into a rhythmic staccato that conveys controlled lethality.

## Layering Notes
- **Attack:** Instant transient — the initial muzzle blast spike
- **Sustain:** Brief (10-20ms) — the crack of the supersonic projectile
- **Decay:** Gradual — 80-100ms of metallic bolt cycling and fading echo
- **Reverb:** Tight, medium-sized room or outdoor environment — not too cavernous
- **Distance modeling:** Low-pass filter and volume roll-off beyond 50m
