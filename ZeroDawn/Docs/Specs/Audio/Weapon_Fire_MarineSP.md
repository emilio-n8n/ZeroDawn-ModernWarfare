# Sound: Marine SP Weapon Fire

## Sound Description
- **Type:** SFX
- **Context:** Plays each time the Marine SP pump-action shotgun is fired. A tactical 12-gauge shotgun with a fire rate of 80 RPM and 8-round tube magazine.
- **Emotional Quality:** Devastating, visceral, room-clearing — each blast conveys overwhelming close-range stopping power.
- **Duration:** 0.30s per shot
- **Tonal Characterization:** The Marine SP produces a deep, rolling "KABOOM" that is wide and diffused — not a single crack like a rifle, but a broad blast of multiple pellets exiting the barrel simultaneously. The report has a rich, complex texture with a pronounced low-frequency thump from the 12-gauge powder load, layered with the scattered hiss of eight pellets cutting through the air. The pump-action mechanism contributes a signature sound: the racking of the forend between shots produces a distinct metallic "schk-chk" that is integral to the shotgun's audio identity. The muzzle blast is wide and room-filling, with a resonant boom that echoes in enclosed spaces. The sound conveys devastating close-range force — each blast is an event that changes the audio landscape of the engagement.

## Layering Notes
- **Attack:** Wide, spreading transient — not a single point source but a dispersed blast
- **Sustain:** Medium (20-30ms) — the sustained propellant burn and pellet spread
- **Decay:** Medium-long — 200-250ms of receding boom with pellet hiss
- **Reverb:** Highly environment-dependent — sounds absolutely devastating indoors with maximum echo
- **Distance modeling:** The spread pattern means the sound has a distinct directional quality up close but becomes more diffuse at range. The pump-action rack should be audible as a distinct layer after each shot
