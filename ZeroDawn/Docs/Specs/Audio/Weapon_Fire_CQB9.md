# Sound: CQB-9 Weapon Fire

## Sound Description
- **Type:** SFX
- **Context:** Plays each time the CQB-9 submachine gun is fired. A compact suppressed PDW-style SMG with a high fire rate of 900 RPM and 35-round magazine.
- **Emotional Quality:** Quick, suppressed, tactical — conveys close-quarters precision and stealthy lethality.
- **Duration:** 0.05s per shot
- **Tonal Characterization:** The CQB-9 produces the quietest and most rapid fire sound in the game — a series of compressed, suppressed "pop-pop-pop" sounds that are distinctly muffled compared to any other weapon. The integrated suppressor reduces the muzzle blast significantly, giving each shot a soft, pneumatic quality — more "thwip" than "crack." The report is dominated by the mechanical sound of the bolt cycling at 900 RPM, producing a rapid, almost buzzing metallic chatter. The suppressed report has a distinctive airy quality — the sound of expanding gases being vented through the suppressor baffles. In full-auto, the CQB-9 produces a unique tearing-canvas sound — a rapid-fire sequence of muted pops that blend into a sustained, breathy staccato. The sound conveys speed and stealth, perfect for the aggressive close-quarters playstyle the weapon enables.

## Layering Notes
- **Attack:** Soft, rounded transient — the suppressor eliminates the sharp muzzle crack
- **Sustain:** Minimal (3-8ms) — the suppressed report is very brief
- **Decay:** Very short — 30-40ms, the mechanical bolt cycling provides the audible tail
- **Reverb:** Minimal — the suppressed report doesn't excite room reverb as much as unsuppressed weapons
- **Distance modeling:** Falls off very quickly — suppressed fire is the hardest to locate directionally. The CQB-9 should be difficult to pinpoint from beyond 30-40 meters, encouraging its use for stealth flanks
