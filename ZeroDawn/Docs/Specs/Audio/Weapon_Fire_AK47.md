# Sound: AK-47 Weapon Fire

## Sound Description
- **Type:** SFX
- **Context:** Plays each time the AK-47 assault rifle is fired. The AK-47 is a high-damage, high-recoil 7.62mm rifle with a fire rate of 680 RPM.
- **Emotional Quality:** Aggressive, primal, intimidating — evokes the raw power and battlefield-proven reputation of the world's most iconic combat rifle.
- **Duration:** 0.18s per shot
- **Tonal Characterization:** A deep, guttural "boom-thud" that is distinctly lower-pitched than the M4A1. The 7.62x39mm round produces a heavy, blunt concussion that resonates in the chest cavity. The report has a rough, growling quality with a pronounced mid-low frequency emphasis. The wood furniture of the rifle contributes a resonant, almost wooden hollow knock layered under the muzzle blast. The bolt carrier group produces a distinct heavy metallic clang that is slower and more deliberate than the M4A1's. In full-auto, the AK-47's fire sounds like a series of heavy, measured thuds — each round a blunt-force statement.

## Layering Notes
- **Attack:** Strong, blunt transient with immediate low-frequency bloom
- **Sustain:** Short (15-25ms) — the heavy crack of the subsonic/supersonic 7.62mm round
- **Decay:** Longer than M4A1 — 150-200ms of receding boom and bolt clatter
- **Reverb:** Open battlefield or outdoor space — benefits from natural echo
- **Distance modeling:** Low-end frequencies carry farther; the deep thud remains audible at longer ranges than the M4A1's higher-pitched crack
